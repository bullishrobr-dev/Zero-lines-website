# Zero Lines Creative Direction Reference

## Brand Summary
- Brand: Zero Lines
- Category: clinical-luxury skincare
- Motto: advanced skincare solutions
- Core belief: the body already has regenerative intelligence; the brand supports and activates it rather than masking concerns

## Desired Brand Feeling
The customer should feel:
- they are choosing intelligence over hype
- they are investing in advanced care, not basic beauty
- they are entering a future-facing skincare system
- they can trust the brand's discipline and credibility

## Visual Language
The Zero Lines world should combine:
- luxury skincare elegance
- advanced science credibility
- premium tech minimalism
- editorial restraint
- commercially strong product presentation

Preferred cues:
- strong negative space
- clean grids
- refined typography
- controlled lighting
- soft but precise shadows
- elegant close-up product imagery
- sculptural presentation
- smooth glass, water, stone, brushed metal, and skin-inspired surfaces

## Packaging Cues From Current Mockups
Treat these as current family anchors:
- aqua or Tiffany-inspired caps and base
- frosted or translucent vessel body
- minimal charcoal or black typography
- subtle metallic detail when useful
- clean, premium cylindrical silhouette
- restrained front-label layout with a calm hierarchy

## Website Tone Cues
The current site framing is premium, clinical, and product-led.

Useful signals from the project:
- `app/layout.tsx` establishes a premium shell with a branded navbar, page transitions, and consistent site framing
- `lib/constants.ts` defines the brand description as clinical-luxury skincare engineered for skin longevity
- the storytelling sections under `components/sections/` lean into science, protocol, technology, and editorial narrative rather than playful beauty marketing

## Creative Do / Don't
Do:
- keep compositions intentional
- make science feel beautiful
- make beauty feel intelligent
- make assets usable for e-commerce and campaigns
- maintain family consistency across SKUs

Don't:
- drift into generic clean-beauty language
- over-style scenes with props
- use beauty tropes that undermine credibility
- make imagery so clinical that it loses desire
