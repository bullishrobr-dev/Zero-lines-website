# Zero Lines Creative Direction Examples

## Example: Mockup Critique
**Objective**
Assess whether the mockup feels premium enough for Zero Lines.

**Available inputs**
- one product mockup
- product name
- short brand description

**Assumptions**
- the current mockup is the family anchor
- the packaging system should remain minimal and clinical-luxury

**Recommended direction**
Keep the cylindrical silhouette and frosted vessel. Increase the sense of precision through cleaner type spacing, slightly calmer contrast, and more controlled metallic detail rather than adding decorative elements.

**Expansion**
Next, test one cleaner label variant and one more sculptural lighting setup.

## Example: Homepage Imagery Brief
**Objective**
Define hero and support visuals for the homepage.

**Available inputs**
- brand positioning
- product lineup
- premium packaging direction

**Assumptions**
- the homepage should feel cinematic but restrained
- the products should remain the central visual asset

**Recommended direction**
Use one hero still life with strong negative space, cool mineral lighting, and premium product scale. Support it with two secondary image families: clinical texture details and protocol-system product compositions.

**Asset list**
- hero still life
- protocol strip image family
- science texture macro
- device plus topical pairing visual

## Example: Launch Campaign Concept
**Objective**
Create a premium launch direction for Zero Lines.

**Inputs**
- brand brief
- current packaging language

**Core concept**
The skin already knows. We restore the signal.

**Visual world**
Future-facing skincare with controlled lighting, mineral textures, translucent surfaces, and elegant product focus.

**Hero assets**
- campaign hero still life
- product cluster image
- serum or syringe feature close-up
- website landing hero crop

## Example: Product-Line Extension Guidance
**Objective**
Extend the packaging family without breaking consistency.

**Existing product logic**
Minimal cylindrical vessels, aqua accents, frosted bodies, restrained front labels.

**Direction**
Keep the silhouette family stable. Differentiate SKUs through label hierarchy, finish variation, and disciplined accent use rather than changing shape aggressively.

**Launch note**
Reserve the most technical visual emphasis for the syringe and refill system to preserve hierarchy across the line.
